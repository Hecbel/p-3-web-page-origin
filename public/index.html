Blackjack

(Game with a skill component)

The objective of blackjack is simple: beat the dealer's hand. How? You need to form a hand total that is as close as possible to 21, without exceeding a total of 21. If your hand goes over 21, then you have busted and have lost the hand. The dynamic of the game is as follows: you receive two cards facing up, while the dealer receives two cards, where only one is facing up. Then the dealer will ask you if you want another card (hit) or not (stay). This decision is a strategic one that will not only depend on your two cards, but also the dealer's face up card. Roulette

(Game of pure chance)

Roulette is the game where you try to guess which number will be chosen when the dealer throws the ball around the numbered wheel. It's truly a game of chance. In Puerto Rico we use the American roulette, also known as a double-zero wheel. This casino game is the only one in which non-value chips are used during the game (don't panic: when finished playing, the dealer will exchange them for value chips). Texas Hold'em Poker

(Game with a skill component)

Unlike other casino games, cardroom texas hold'em poker does not pit the player against the casino. Instead, players compete against each other to win. The game is played with a 52 card deck at a time. The player who has the better combination of five cards is the winner, but to get to five cards you need to bet, either because you know you can obtain a winning combination or because you are simply bluffing. The money won or lost is transferred from one player to another. The dealer provided by the casino does not play and charges a percentage for every hand, which is called the rake. A rake of 5% is the typical percentage used in P.R. casinos. Learn more by clicking the links below! References

Mini-Baccarat

(Game of pure chance)

While 21 is the magic number in blackjack, in baccarat it is the number 9. The winning hand is the hand closest to 9. In my opinion, this is the easiest table game available. A baccarat game offers a choice of only two basic different bets: banker and player. Keep in mind that in this specific game, player is the bet and NOT you. So, this means you are going to bet either on the banker or on the player. Your bet represents that you believe that the banker or the player will win. You have a 50/50 chance of winning, like if you were throwing a quarter in the air and betting on heads or tails. In Puerto Rico, baccarat is played with eight decks and it is truly a game of pure chance. Learn more by clicking the links below! References
